## Aotearoa and region context
The AWS Asia Pacific (New Zealand) Region launched in Auckland in
August 2025 — the first AWS infrastructure region in Aotearoa,
with three Availability Zones. This is a significant milestone for
NZ builders, particularly for data residency requirements in
government and iwi workloads.

However, AgentCore is not yet available in the Auckland region
(ap-southeast-4). The closest supported AgentCore region is
Asia Pacific (Sydney) — ap-southeast-2.

This means builders in Aotearoa are in an interesting position:
we have a local AWS region, but the most advanced agentic services
still run out of Sydney. That gap is worth naming honestly in
content — it is a real and current constraint for NZ builders,
not a limitation to hide.

Watch for AgentCore expanding to ap-southeast-4 — that will be
a significant content moment worth covering immediately.

## The Aotearoa angle — what it means in practice
When it appears in content, the Aotearoa angle should answer:
"Why does this specific thing matter to builders in Aotearoa
or the wider Oceania community right now?"

Relevant dimensions:
- **Region reality** — building in Aotearoa, deploying to Sydney.
  The Auckland region exists but AgentCore isn't there yet.
- **Time zone isolation** — re:Invent happens while NZ is asleep.
  AWS Summits are in Sydney or Auckland, not around the corner.
  Staying current requires more deliberate effort here, which is
  literally why the Magic Content Engine exists.
- **Community scale** — AWSUG Aotearoa is tight-knit. Things that
  land differently for a small, distributed Pacific community than
  they do for a 50,000-person meetup in Seattle.
- **Data residency** — the Auckland region launch matters for
  government and iwi workloads. AgentCore services not yet
  available there is a genuine gap worth naming.
- **Partnership context** — Datacom (Paul Dunlop, Head of AWS)
  was a launch partner for the Auckland region. NZ enterprise
  adoption of AgentCore is a live story.

The Aotearoa angle is optional — only include it when genuinely
relevant, not as a formula. One honest paragraph, ~50-80 words.
Never forced. Never performative.

## Voice rules
- Conversational, first-person, honest about gaps in knowledge
- No em-dashes
- Short sentences preferred
- Do not open with "I"
- "Kia ora" or "Ngā mihi" for NZ or government-facing content
- Never use: leverage, empower, unlock, dive into, game-changer
- Reference Aotearoa/Pacific community context naturally
- Never write the hook, cold open, personal notes, or closing —
  those <!-- MIKE: --> placeholders belong to Mike
